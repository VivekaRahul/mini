Color palettes and interior design go hand in hand to create cohesive and visually appealing spaces. A well-chosen color palette can set the mood, create harmony, and enhance the overall aesthetic of an interior design. Here are some tips to consider when working with color palettes in interior design

Understand the Basics: Familiarize yourself with the color wheel, which consists of primary, secondary, and tertiary colors. Complementary colors (opposite on the color wheel) create high contrast, while analogous colors (next to each other) offer a harmonious feel. Monochromatic color schemes use variations of a single color for a sophisticated look. Choose a Dominant Color: Start by selecting a dominant color for your space. This color will have the most significant impact and will set the overall tone. Consider the desired mood and the function of the room. For example, blues and greens are calming, while yellows and oranges create a vibrant atmosphere. Use Accent Colors: Select a few accent colors to complement the dominant color and add visual interest. Accents can be incorporated through furniture, accessories, artwork, or accent walls. Experiment with different combinations to find what works best for your design. Consider the 60-30-10 Rule: To maintain balance, use the 60-30-10 rule when applying colors. The dominant color should cover about 60% of the space (walls, large furniture), the secondary color about 30% (upholstery, rugs), and the accent color about 10% (decorative accessories, artwork). Pay Attention to Lighting: Lighting can significantly affect how colors appear in a space. Consider both natural and artificial lighting when selecting colors. Test samples under different lighting conditions to ensure the desired effect. Create Flow: In an open floor plan or connected spaces, ensure a cohesive flow by using a consistent color palette throughout. This will help create a sense of continuity and unity in your design.
